Etsy listing photo sizes 2026: the complete guide

Exact size requirements, optimal dimensions, aspect ratios, and the rules that actually affect search rankings.

Michael Smyth
June 17, 2026
TL;DR

Etsy listing photo minimum is 2,000 pixels on the shortest side. Optimal is 3,000 × 3,000 (square) or 3,000 × 2,250 (4:3). Maximum file size is 10 MB per image. First photo must be at least 635 px to avoid lower search ranking. JPEG or PNG, sRGB color space. Ignore anything older than 2024 — Etsy revised the recommendations.

Etsy's photo size rules have changed a few times in recent years, and most "Etsy photo size 2022" guides you'll find are outdated. This is the current 2026 reality: what Etsy actually requires, what it recommends, and what happens if you ignore the rules.

What size should you actually upload?

If you only remember one number: 3,000 × 3,000 pixels, saved as a JPEG under 1 MB. That single spec covers Etsy's recommendation for square format, looks sharp on Retina and 4K screens, enables smooth zoom on desktop, and uploads quickly.

If you prefer a wider format for your products (flat lays, horizontal items), 3,000 × 2,250 (4:3) matches Etsy's display ratio in search results. Either choice is fine; square is more universal across contexts.

Why the first photo is special

Your first listing photo (the "primary" or "hero" photo) is what shows up in search results as the thumbnail. Buyers click or skip based on it. Etsy explicitly states the first photo needs to be at least 635 pixels wide — below that, the listing ranks lower in search.

In practice, if you follow the 2,000 px minimum across all photos, the first photo will be well above 635 px automatically. The 635 px rule exists specifically to catch shops that upload tiny thumbnails as the primary image. Don't be that shop.

Aspect ratio: square vs 4:3

Etsy displays search results in a 4:3 ratio. If you upload a square image (1:1), Etsy crops the top and bottom in search. If you upload a 4:3 image, no cropping.

Depending on your product, this matters more or less:

  • Jewelry, small items: square works fine because the product is centered and the cropping doesn't lose anything
  • Wall art, flat lays, wide products: 4:3 is safer — square crops lose important content
  • Digital downloads with preview mockups: design the mockup for 4:3 to guarantee search-result appearance

Still uploading square for everything? You'll be fine — Etsy's algorithm doesn't penalize aspect ratio choice. The crop is a cosmetic concern, not a ranking one.

Maximum file size and format

10 MB is the hard cap. A properly optimized 3,000 × 3,000 JPEG comes in around 500 KB to 2 MB, so hitting 10 MB usually means something's wrong — uncompressed TIFF, raw PNG, or an unnecessarily high quality setting.

JPEG vs PNG:

  • JPEG — best for photographs, product shots, lifestyle scenes. Smaller file size, acceptable quality loss from compression.
  • PNG — best for graphics with sharp edges, text, transparency. Larger file size, lossless quality.

For most Etsy product photos, JPEG is the right call. PNG only if your product is a graphic or the image has transparent backgrounds you need to preserve (digital products sometimes fit this).

What happens if your photos are too small

Two failure modes:

  1. Under 2,000 px on any dimension: Etsy shows a warning on the listing editor. The listing still saves, but quality concerns affect the buyer experience — no zoom on desktop, pixelated on Retina screens.
  2. First photo under 635 px: Etsy specifically lowers your search ranking. This is the one size error with direct SEO consequences.

If your images come from an old Dropbox folder or outdated mockup, they may be 800 or 1,200 px. Those will trigger warnings. Upscale or replace before uploading.

Bulk resizing workflow

For sellers with many listings or a drop coming up, resize images before you start listing, not during. Trying to fix sizes during the Etsy form is the worst use of your time.

Three practical approaches:

  • Photoshop batch action: record an action that resizes to 3,000 × 3,000 with JPEG quality 80, run it across a folder
  • Online tools: ImResizer, Bulk Image Resizer, Canva's resize function
  • Mac Automator or Windows PowerToys: both include built-in batch image resize

The recommendations that aren't rules

Beyond size, Etsy recommends but doesn't enforce:

  • sRGB color space — if your images look off-color on Etsy, this is usually why. Convert in your editor before upload.
  • Well-lit, neutral backgrounds — not technically a rule, but conversion data consistently shows this beats cluttered lifestyle shots for discoverability
  • Consistent style across listings — makes your shop look professional in search results
  • At least 5 photos per listing — Etsy allows 10, and listings with 5+ convert better

The size rules summarized for 2026

If you leave this page with just one cheat-sheet entry, make it this:

3,000 × 3,000 JPEG, sRGB, under 1 MB, minimum 5 photos per listing, first photo well above 635 px. Resize before uploading, never during.

That spec works for square or 4:3 displays, meets Etsy's recommendations for 2026, renders sharp on modern screens, and avoids any size-related ranking penalties. Every other variation is just a tradeoff against that baseline.

Frequently asked questions

What size should Etsy listing photos be in 2026?

Etsy recommends listing photos be at least 2,000 pixels on the shortest side, with 3,000 × 3,000 as the optimal upload size. Maximum file size is 10 MB per image.

What aspect ratio do Etsy listing photos use?

Etsy displays listing photos at 4:3 in search results. Uploading at 3,000 × 3,000 (1:1) or 3,000 × 2,250 (4:3) both work well.

Does the first Etsy listing photo need to be a different size?

The first photo needs to be at least 635 pixels wide or your listing can rank lower in search. Following the 2,000 px rule for all photos automatically satisfies this.

What's the maximum file size for an Etsy photo?

10 MB per image. Keep file size under 1 MB if possible for faster uploads.
