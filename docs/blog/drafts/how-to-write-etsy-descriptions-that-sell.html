How to write Etsy descriptions that sell (2026 guide + templates)

A practical framework for writing descriptions that rank in search and convert browsers to buyers. With copy-paste templates.

Michael Smyth
July 3, 2026
TL;DR

Etsy descriptions should be 300-600 words, lead with what the product is and who it's for in the first 160 characters, use scannable bullet-point format for features, include your primary keyword naturally (no stuffing), and end with what the buyer actually gets (file formats for digital, size/materials for physical). Templates below. AI can draft 80% of this — just review and edit.

Etsy descriptions do two jobs simultaneously — signal relevance to Etsy's search algorithm, and convince a browsing buyer to click Add to Cart. Most sellers write as if only the second job exists (dense lyrical paragraphs), or only the first (keyword-stuffed strings of phrases). Neither works. The descriptions that actually sell do both at the same time, using a specific structure.

This article is that structure, plus templates you can copy.

The 3-second hook

When a buyer lands on your listing page, their eye goes to the photo first, then the title, then the first visible line of the description. You have about 3 seconds before they decide whether to scroll or click away.

The first 160 characters of your description are those 3 seconds. That's also exactly what Etsy's search results preview shows to buyers who hover over listings. Treat those 160 characters as a miniature sales page.

Bad first lines:

  • "Welcome to my shop!" (wastes space, tells buyers nothing)
  • "This beautiful piece was carefully crafted..." (no keyword, no specificity)
  • "Thank you for viewing my listing." (thank-you is for the bottom, not the top)

Good first lines:

  • "Editable Canva wedding invitation template for DIY brides — modern boho aesthetic, 5x7 printable, instant download."
  • "Sunset mountain SVG cut file for Cricut and Silhouette — perfect for shirts, decals, and wall art. Commercial license included."
  • "Minimalist daily planner PDF — GoodNotes compatible, undated, 30 pages, A5 and letter formats."

The pattern: what it is, who it's for, what's included. Clear in 15 seconds of reading.

The full structure

After the hook, use this structure for the rest of the description:

  1. Hook (1-2 sentences, 160 chars) — what it is, who it's for
  2. Short paragraph (2-3 sentences) — expand on the benefit. Why should they care?
  3. Features (bullet list) — specifics. File format, dimensions, what's included.
  4. How to use / how you receive (short section) — what happens after purchase
  5. Important notes (short section) — licensing, sizing, compatibility caveats
  6. Close (1-2 sentences) — thank-you, encouragement to message with questions

Total: 300-600 words. Enough to answer buyer questions and give Etsy's algorithm text to index, short enough that actual humans read it.

The keywords game in descriptions

Etsy's algorithm weighs description text as a ranking signal, but less heavily than the title and tags. The practical implication: use keywords naturally, not at all costs. A description that reads like a robot wrote it kills conversion even if it ranks marginally better.

Rules for description keywords:

  • Include your primary keyword once in the first 160 characters
  • Include it again naturally in the body (1-2 more times)
  • Include 2-3 related long-tail phrases in the body naturally
  • Don't force keywords into bullet points where they don't fit
  • Don't include phrases like "buy [keyword] online" or "best [keyword] Etsy" — these read as spam to both humans and algorithms

What actually moves conversion

Based on patterns across many Etsy shops:

Specificity beats poetry. "5×7 inch printable wedding invitation with 2 matching RSVP cards" converts better than "Beautiful wedding stationery that will make your special day unforgettable." Buyers know what they want; specificity helps them confirm it.

Bullet lists beat dense paragraphs. 70%+ of buyers skim. Bullets give them what they need in one pass.

Address the obvious question. For digital products, include "instant download" — buyers filter for it and won't click if unclear. For personalized items, include "personalization takes X days" so they know what to expect. For physical items, mention processing time even if it's also in the Etsy-standard field.

The close doesn't need to be elaborate. A single line thanking the buyer and offering to answer questions is enough. Long "about my brand" closing paragraphs don't add conversion.

Common mistakes that kill descriptions

Burying the hook under a greeting. "Welcome to my shop! I'm so glad you're here. I make beautiful things with love..." By the time you get to the product, buyers have scrolled past.

Keyword stuffing. "Beautiful wedding invitation wedding stationery wedding template modern boho wedding invite printable wedding wedding digital download wedding wedding wedding..." Etsy's algorithm doesn't reward this and buyers notice.

Walls of text. 500 words in one paragraph is invisible. Same 500 words in a hook + bullet list + close is usable.

Copying descriptions across listings. If all your listings have identical descriptions with just the product name swapped, Etsy may flag it as low-quality content. Vary the wording even if the structure is the same.

Forgetting the download instructions. For digital products, telling buyers how to download is the #1 question in customer support. Include it in the description upfront.

The shortest actionable advice

If you leave this article with only one change:

Rewrite the first 160 characters of your top 10 listings right now. Replace "Welcome to my shop!" and "This beautiful piece..." with "What it is, who it's for." Measure the traffic to those listings over the next 30 days. Most sellers who do this see a real lift — and it takes 15 minutes total.

The rest of the description structure matters, but the first 160 characters is where you win or lose the 3-second hook. Fix that first; everything else is iteration.

Frequently asked questions

How long should an Etsy description be?

300-600 words is the sweet spot. Long enough to answer buyer questions, short enough that buyers read it.

What should you put in the first line of an Etsy description?

What the product is and who it's for, within 160 characters. That's what Etsy and search engines preview.

Should Etsy descriptions include keywords?

Yes, naturally. Include your primary keyword once in the first 160 characters and again naturally in the body. Don't keyword-stuff.
