BulkListingPro vs Easy Listing Uploader: which one fits your workflow?

Two Etsy bulk tools that look similar on the surface but take different approaches under the hood. Here's the honest side-by-side.

Michael Smyth
April 18, 2026
TL;DR

Easy Listing Uploader is a template-driven Etsy bulk tool with a subscription model. BulkListingPro is an AI-first Etsy bulk tool with per-listing credit pricing. Pick Easy Listing Uploader if you list continuously and want deterministic template control. Pick BulkListingPro if you list in drops and want AI to handle the title and tag writing.

Easy Listing Uploader has been part of the Etsy bulk tool landscape for years. BulkListingPro is newer. If you've been researching Etsy bulk upload options, these two have probably both shown up in your search results, and you're trying to figure out which one fits.

Here's the short version: they're solving the same top-level problem — "don't make me type every listing into Etsy's form by hand" — but they approach it from different angles. The right tool depends on how you want to do the writing, how often you list, and whether you're okay paying a monthly fee during weeks you don't ship anything.

What they share

Before the differences, the overlap is worth naming because it's real:

  • Both are Etsy-only. Neither cross-lists to eBay, Poshmark, or Facebook Marketplace.
  • Both accept CSV and XLSX imports.
  • Both automate Etsy's new-listing form — you stay logged in, the tool fills the fields.
  • Both handle digital product files (PDF, ZIP, SVG, etc.).
  • Both support multiple images per listing.

The core job is the same. What diverges is how they get there.

Where they diverge

Template control vs AI drafting

Easy Listing Uploader is built around templates. You define a title pattern like "{product} - {style} - Digital Download for {use_case}", populate the placeholders from your spreadsheet columns, and the tool fills Etsy's title field accordingly. You are in charge of the words. The tool is in charge of the repetition.

BulkListingPro is built around AI generation. You can leave title, tags, and description blank in your CSV, click "Generate All," and AI drafts all three per listing using the image and category as input. You review the output and edit as needed. The AI is in charge of the first draft; you're in charge of the final pass.

Neither approach is categorically better. Templates win when your products have a clean, repeatable structure. AI wins when your products vary in ways that don't fit a fill-in-the-blank.

Subscription vs credits

Easy Listing Uploader offers a free plan plus paid subscription tiers, and it's API-approved by Etsy. Its capacity goes up to 3,000 listings per upload, making it a serious choice for large drops. BulkListingPro uses credit packs, starting at $1.99 for 50 credits (~25 listings). No subscription, credits don't expire, you pay only for listings you upload.

If your listing volume is steady month to month, a subscription is predictable and usually cheaper per listing. If it's lumpy — a drop of 100 listings, then quiet for six weeks — credits work in your favor.

One feature worth calling out specifically: Easy Listing Uploader can also download your existing Etsy listings into a CSV file. That's genuinely useful for audits, migration, or editing shop-wide data in a spreadsheet. It also ships with a built-in Etsy sales and fees calculator. BulkListingPro is focused on the creation-side workflow and doesn't offer existing-listing export.

Interface style

Easy Listing Uploader has traditionally operated with a desktop application feel. BulkListingPro is a Chrome extension with a sidepanel and a full-page editor. The practical difference: the extension stays out of the way when you're not using it; the desktop app asks for more ceremony to launch and manage.

Who should pick which

Pick Easy Listing Uploader if:

  • Your products follow a predictable template pattern
  • You list consistently every week
  • You prefer writing your own titles and tags over editing AI drafts
  • Subscription billing fits your pattern better than pay-as-you-go

Pick BulkListingPro if:

  • You list in drops — seasonal, themed, or new-collection batches
  • You'd rather edit AI drafts than write every title by hand
  • Your products are varied enough that a single template doesn't fit
  • You want to pay per listing with no monthly commitment
Neither tool is the "better" one. They optimize for different working styles, and the way you already list will tell you which one to use within a weekend of trying both.

The verdict

If you've been happy with Easy Listing Uploader's template approach and the subscription cost makes sense for your volume, there's no urgent reason to switch. If you're a drop-based seller who's been resisting the monthly fee or struggling to maintain a hundred title templates, BulkListingPro is built around your complaint.

Both tools have free starts. The cleanest way to decide is to run the same batch of ten products through each and see which workflow you prefer. Feel beats any comparison table.

Frequently asked questions

Which is cheaper, BulkListingPro or Easy Listing Uploader?

It depends on listing cadence. BulkListingPro charges per listing via credit packs starting at $1.99, with no subscription — best for sellers who list in seasonal drops. Easy Listing Uploader follows a subscription-tier model, which is more economical if you list every week all year.

Do both tools support digital products?

Yes. Both BulkListingPro and Easy Listing Uploader handle digital file uploads (PDF, ZIP, SVG, etc.) and are regularly used by SVG, planner, and printable sellers.

Does Easy Listing Uploader generate titles and tags with AI?

Easy Listing Uploader has historically been a template-driven tool — you provide the text, the tool fills it into Etsy's form. BulkListingPro is AI-first: you can leave titles, tags, and descriptions blank and let AI draft them from the image and category.

Can I migrate my spreadsheet from Easy Listing Uploader to BulkListingPro?

Yes, with column re-mapping. Both tools accept CSV/XLSX, but the column names and required fields differ. Export your data from Easy Listing Uploader, adjust the headers to match the BulkListingPro template, and re-import.
