BulkListingPro vs SKUpid: which Etsy bulk uploader fits you?

Two Etsy bulk upload tools with very different monetization and content philosophies. Here's an honest side-by-side.

Michael Smyth
April 18, 2026
TL;DR

Both are Etsy-only bulk uploaders. SKUpid has a generous free tier (unlimited uploads, but images must be added manually on Etsy afterward) and a $19.95/month Pro plan that automates images. BulkListingPro skips subscriptions entirely — credit packs from $1.99 — and adds AI-generated titles, tags, and descriptions. Pick SKUpid if "free with manual image steps" works for your volume. Pick BulkListingPro if you want AI content and per-listing pricing.

SKUpid and BulkListingPro solve the same top-level problem: "don't make me click Etsy's new-listing form a hundred times." They're both Etsy-only, both spreadsheet-driven, both automate listing creation. Where they diverge is in how they charge and how much content they help you produce.

If you've been comparing them and the features looked similar at first glance, this is the detail that actually matters.

What each tool does

SKUpid is a bulk upload tool built around a simple promise: let sellers get many listings into Etsy without paying a traditional subscription to do it. The free tier is genuinely free — unlimited CSV uploads — with one real constraint: images have to be added manually on Etsy after the listings are created. The Pro tier ($19.95/month, or $199.95/year) removes that constraint and adds image automation, translations, and editing features.

BulkListingPro is a bulk upload tool built around a different promise: skip the subscription, pay per listing, and let AI write the titles and tags. Credit packs start at $1.99 for 50 credits (roughly 25 listings at 2 credits each) and go up to $24.99 for 1000 credits (~500 listings). Credits don't expire and there's no monthly commitment. AI drafts content from the image and category so you're not writing titles a hundred times.

Different monetization. Different content approach. Same underlying job.

The pricing math reveals the tradeoff:

  • For drops and lumpy listing patterns, BulkListingPro's credits are dramatically cheaper over a year
  • For heavy continuous use, SKUpid's Pro tier (unlimited uploads + images at $19.95/mo) can be cheaper if volume is very high

The content generation difference

Equally important: what the tool helps you produce. SKUpid is pass-through for content — you provide the title, tags, description, and other fields in your spreadsheet, and SKUpid pushes them to Etsy. BulkListingPro adds AI drafting on top — you can leave those fields blank and have AI generate them per listing based on the image and category.

If you already have a template or a library of titles and tags that you're happy with, SKUpid's pass-through model is clean. If you're staring at 100 blank rows and don't want to write 100 titles by hand, BulkListingPro's AI layer saves meaningful time.

The writing is where big drops burn you out. SKUpid is a fast pipe for content you already have. BulkListingPro is a pipe plus a drafter for when you don't.

Who should pick which

Pick SKUpid if:

  • You already write your own titles, tags, and descriptions and just need a faster way to push them to Etsy
  • You're okay manually attaching images on Etsy (free tier) or willing to pay $19.95/mo for Pro to automate them
  • You list continuously every month and a subscription fits your rhythm
  • Multi-language translation is important to you

Pick BulkListingPro if:

  • You don't want to write titles and tags by hand — you want AI to draft them
  • You list in drops and don't want to pay monthly between drops
  • You prefer per-listing pricing that tracks your actual usage
  • Your digital products need full-pipeline automation including images

The verdict

Both tools are legitimate answers to Etsy's missing bulk-upload feature. The decision is rarely about which is "better" — it's about matching the tool's philosophy to yours. SKUpid trusts you to write your own content and rewards you with a free tier. BulkListingPro trusts AI to draft the content and charges you per listing in exchange for that drafting labor.

If you'd rather save money and write more, SKUpid. If you'd rather write less and let the tool draft, BulkListingPro. The product differences flow from that single philosophical split.

Frequently asked questions

Is SKUpid really free?

SKUpid has a genuine free tier with unlimited uploads, but images have to be added manually on Etsy after the listings are created. The Pro plan at $19.95/month adds automated image handling plus translations and editing.

Which tool has better content generation for Etsy listings?

BulkListingPro has AI-generated titles, tags, and descriptions as a primary feature. SKUpid is spreadsheet-driven pass-through: you provide the content, the tool uploads it.

What are the pricing models?

SKUpid uses subscription with a free tier ($0/month, manual images) and Pro ($19.95/month or $199.95/year). BulkListingPro uses credit packs starting at $1.99 with no subscription.

Do both tools support digital products?

Yes. Both SKUpid and BulkListingPro handle Etsy digital product listings with digital file uploads. Both are Etsy-only.
